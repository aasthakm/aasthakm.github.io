Overview

A large number of software applications store and process valuable data. These applications are the target of a variety of attacks. One type of attacks are side-channel attacks. Side channels arise in system resources that are shared among mutually untrusting principals. Side-channel attacks are becoming increasingly sophisticated, while building efficient defenses is challenging. This course will give an overview of software-based side-channel attacks. We will study papers that describe side channels in resources, such as caches, memory, CPUs, GPUs, and network, and how attacks are designed to exploit the side channels. We will also study state-of-the-art solutions to mitigate various side channels, which include techniques at the architecture, the OS, languages and compilers, and the application level.

Course structure and grading

The course is structured as a seminar. Every class, two students will present a paper each, which will then be discussed in the class. Before each class, all students are expected to read the paper, prepare a list of discussion questions, and write a short critical review of the paper. There will be one or two assignments (first half of the term), and an exploratory project component.

Grading (tentative, subject to change until the beginning of the course)

  • Paper reviews: 20%
  • Presentation: 20%
  • Class participation: 20%
  • Assignments: 20%
  • Project: 20%

The student presenting a paper need not write a review for the presented paper. Top 20 reviews will be considered for grading.
